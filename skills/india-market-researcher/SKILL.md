- **Promoters**: Typically hold 40–70% in Indian companies; pledge data is critical risk signal

- Circuit filters: 10%, 15%, 20% index-level; individual stock upper/lower circuits (5%, 10%, 20%)

## Fundamental Analysis — India Context

### Company Analysis Framework

1. **Promoter Quality & Governance**: Shareholding pattern, promoter pledge %, related party transactions, audit quality (Big 4 vs unknown), AGM resolutions
2. **Business Model & Moat**: Is the moat defensible in India's price-sensitive market? Distribution reach, brand loyalty, regulatory moat (banking licenses, spectrum)
3. **Revenue & Earnings Quality**: Watch for aggressive revenue recognition, high receivables days, and frequent write-offs
4. **Working Capital Efficiency**: Debtors days, creditors days, inventory days — crucial in manufacturing and trading companies
5. **Capital Allocation**: ROCE > cost of capital consistently? Dividend track record? Buyback history?
6. **Debt & Liquidity**: Net Debt/EBITDA < 2x preferred; watch for churn in short-term borrowings

### India-Specific Valuation Metrics

| Metric | India Usage | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| P/E | Universal | Compare to Nifty P/E (historical avg ~20–22x) |
| EV/EBITDA | Industrials, infra, telecom | Preferred over P/E for capital-heavy |
| P/B | Banks, NBFCs, financials | ROE context essential; RoA for banks |
| P/S | New-age tech, high-growth | Pair with revenue growth rate |
| Mcap/Sales | FMCG, consumer | Brand premium justification |
| Dividend Yield | PSUs, mature cos | PSU divestment plays add yield catalyst |
| EV/Reserves | Oil & Gas, mining | Asset-heavy sectors |

### Key Domestic Indicators

| Indicator | Source | Frequency | Market Impact |
|-----------|--------|-----------|---------------|
| RBI Repo Rate | RBI MPC | 6x/year | Rate-sensitive sectors: banks, NBFCs, realty |

| PMI Manufacturing/Services | S&P Global | Monthly | Business confidence |

| FII/DII Flows | NSDL/BSE | Daily | Institutional market direction |
| India VIX | NSE | Daily | Fear gauge; >20 = elevated stress |

### RBI & Monetary Policy Impact
- **Rate hike cycle**: NBFCs, housing finance companies (HFCs), realty stocks derate; bank NIMs widen short-term
- **Rate cut cycle**: Rate-sensitive sectors re-rate; auto, realty, HFCs benefit; bond yields fall
- **Liquidity (CRR/SLR/OMO)**: System liquidity drives credit growth and bank profitability
- **INR depreciation**: IT/software exporters benefit (revenue in USD); importers (oil, gold, electronics) hurt
- **INR appreciation**: Import-heavy sectors benefit; export-oriented sectors face headwinds

### Union Budget — Annual Market Event
- **Date**: February 1 every year (presented in Parliament)
- **Key watch areas**: Capital expenditure (infra push), tax slabs (consumption boost), fiscal deficit target, sector-specific incentives (PLI schemes, import duties), divestment targets
- **Typical Budget plays**: Defence, railways, roads/infra, affordable housing, agriculture, MSMEs
- **Post-budget sectors to track**: Changes in customs duty on imports/exports directly impact margins

### Monsoon & Agri Economy
- India's agricultural sector: ~15% of GDP; >45% of employment
- Normal monsoon (96–104% of LPA) = positive for rural FMCG, tractors, fertilizers, agri-inputs, MFIs
- Deficit monsoon = rural stress, food inflation, RBI policy complication
- **Kharif season** (June–October): Rice, cotton, soybean — dependent on southwest monsoon
- **Rabi season** (November–March): Wheat, mustard — dependent on winter rain and irrigation

## Sector Analysis — India Specific

### Banking & Financial Services (Largest Nifty weight ~35%)
- Track: Credit growth, NIM trends, asset quality (GNPA/NNPA), CASA ratio, PCR
- Private banks vs PSU banks: Private = quality + growth; PSU = value + dividend + government capex beneficiary
- NBFCs: Watch ALM (asset-liability mismatch), borrowing cost trends, AUM growth
- Key triggers: RBI rate decisions, credit policy, liquidity measures, NPA resolution (IBC cases)
- Stocks to model: HDFC Bank, ICICI Bank, Kotak, Axis, SBI, Bank of Baroda, Bajaj Finance

### Information Technology (Nifty weight ~13%)
- Revenue in USD; INR depreciation = margin tailwind
- Track: Revenue growth (CC — constant currency basis), deal wins (TCV), attrition rate, EBIT margin
- US recession risk = demand slowdown in BFSI, retail verticals
- BFSI + Healthcare + Manufacturing = top verticals; GenAI services = emerging growth driver
- Key triggers: US GDP, USD/INR, client budget cuts, deal ramp-up timing
- Stocks: TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCL Tech, Tech Mahindra, LTIMindtree

### FMCG & Consumer
- Rural vs urban volume split — rural recovery is key thesis for mid-size FMCG
- Track: Volume growth (not value — strips out price hikes), gross margin (raw material: palm oil, crude derivatives, packaging)
- GST rationalization = margin/volume catalyst
- Premiumization trend in urban India — entry point for premium brands
- Stocks: HUL, ITC, Nestle India, Britannia, Dabur, Marico, Godrej Consumer

### Automobile & Auto Ancillaries
- Monthly wholesale + retail (FADA) data is the pulse check
- EV transition: Watch Tata Motors (domestic EV), M&M (EV SUVs); traditional OEMs' EV capex
- PLI scheme for auto components = domestic manufacturing tailwind
- Commodity prices (steel, aluminum, copper) directly impact margins
- Two-wheelers = rural demand proxy; PVs = urban middle class; CVs = infrastructure activity
- Stocks: Maruti, Tata Motors, M&M, Bajaj Auto, Hero MotoCorp, Eicher, TVS Motor

### Infrastructure, Capital Goods & Defence
- Biggest government Budget beneficiary; track capex allocation YoY
- Order book size, book-to-bill ratio, execution pace = key metrics
- Defence indigenization (Atmanirbhar Bharat): HAL, BEL, Bharat Forge, MTAR, Paras Defence
- Infra: L&T, KNR Constructions, PNC Infratech, IRB Infra
- Railways: RVNL, IRFC, Titagarh Rail Systems
- Power: NTPC, Power Grid, Torrent Power, CESC

### Pharma & Healthcare
- Domestic formulations: Stable, India pricing-regulated; NLEM (essential medicines list) risk
- US generics: USFDA inspection outcomes = binary event risk; ANDA filings, Para IV wins
- API (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients): China+1 beneficiary; track China dependence
- CDMO: Contract manufacturing — growing secular theme (Divi's, Laurus Labs, Syngene)
- Hospitals: Apollo, Fortis, Aster — ARPOB (revenue per occupied bed) + occupancy
- Stocks: Sun Pharma, Dr Reddy's, Cipla, Aurobindo, Divi's, Alkem, Torrent Pharma

### Real Estate
- RBI rate cycle most sensitive sector — affordability-driven
- RERA compliance = trust builder; track project delivery timelines
- Track: Pre-sales/bookings (GDV), collections, net debt reduction, new launches
- Luxury vs affordable: Different demand drivers; luxury = wealth effect; affordable = rate sensitive
- Stocks: DLF, Macrotech (Lodha), Godrej Properties, Prestige, Sobha

## Technical Analysis — Indian Markets

### Nifty 50 Key Levels Framework
- Always identify: 52-week high/low, all-time high (ATH), major consolidation zones
- Nifty historical P/E band: 15–18x = undervalued; 20–22x = fair value; >25x = stretched
- India VIX correlation: VIX rising + Nifty falling = panic; VIX falling + Nifty rising = healthy uptrend

### F&O Data Analysis (India-specific edge)
- **PCR (Put-Call Ratio)**: >1.2 = bullish sentiment; <0.8 = bearish; extremes = contrarian signal
- **Max Pain**: Strike where option writers profit most — expiry gravitates toward max pain
- **OI (Open Interest) Analysis**: Rising OI + rising price = strong uptrend; rising OI + falling price = strong downtrend
- **OI Unwinding**: Falling OI + falling price = long unwinding (bearish); falling OI + rising price = short covering (bullish)
- **IV (Implied Volatility)**: Rising IV = uncertainty ahead; falling IV = calm; compare to India VIX
- **FII Index Futures positioning**: Long/short buildup in Nifty/BankNifty futures = directional signal

### Sector Rotation in Indian Context
- FII buying into large-caps first → then midcaps → then small-caps (risk-on progression)
- RBI rate cuts → banks, NBFCs, realty, auto re-rate first
- Strong monsoon → FMCG, agri, rural consumption stocks
- Budget capex push → infra, defence, railways, capital goods
- INR weakness → IT exporters, pharma exporters

### Backtesting — India-Specific Considerations
- Use NSE/BSE adjusted price data (accounting for dividends, splits, bonuses)

- LTCG (>12 months): 10% tax on gains >₹1 lakh; STCG: 15% — factor into post-tax returns
- Earnings blackout periods: No trading on insider information (SEBI PIT Regulations)
