## Fundamental Analysis

### Company Analysis Framework
1. **Business Model**: How does the company make money? Moat? Pricing power?
2. **Revenue Quality**: Recurring vs one-time, diversified vs concentrated, organic vs acquired
3. **Profitability**: Gross margin trend, operating leverage, EBITDA margin, net margin
4. **Balance Sheet Health**: Debt/Equity, Interest Coverage Ratio, Current Ratio, Cash Runway
5. **Cash Flow**: FCF yield, FCF conversion (FCF/Net Income), capex intensity
6. **Management Quality**: Capital allocation track record, insider ownership, compensation alignment

### Key Valuation Metrics

| Metric | Use Case | What to Watch |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| P/E | Mature companies | Compare to sector & historical avg |
| Forward P/E | Growth companies | Estimate reliability matters |
| EV/EBITDA | Capital-heavy businesses | Strips out leverage |
| P/S | High-growth, pre-profit | Paired with growth rate |
| PEG Ratio | Growth at reasonable price | PEG < 1 historically attractive |
| P/FCF | Cash generative businesses | More reliable than earnings |
| EV/FCF | Capital structure agnostic | Best for comparables |
| Price/Book | Banks, financials | ROE context required |

### Earnings Analysis
- Surprise magnitude and direction vs. consensus estimates (EPS and Revenue)
- Guidance revision: raised / maintained / lowered — guidance > actuals for price action
- Analyst estimate revision momentum (ERMs): upgrades cluster = bullish signal
- Beat-and-raise cadence: most powerful re-rating catalyst
- Quality of beat: organic growth vs. cost cuts vs. buybacks

### Competitive Moat Assessment (Porter's 5 Forces + Moat Types)
- Network effects, switching costs, cost advantages, intangible assets, efficient scale
- TAM / SAM / SOM sizing for growth companies
- Market share trend — gaining or losing?

## Technical Analysis

### Trend Analysis
- **Primary trend**: 200-day SMA — price above = bullish regime; below = bearish
- **Intermediate trend**: 50-day SMA for medium-term direction
- **Short-term trend**: 20-day EMA for swing trading setups
- **Trend strength**: ADX > 25 = trending; < 20 = ranging

### Key Chart Patterns
**Bullish**: Cup & Handle, Ascending Triangle, Bull Flag, Double Bottom, Inverse Head & Shoulders
**Bearish**: Head & Shoulders, Descending Triangle, Bear Flag, Double Top, Rising Wedge
**Continuation**: Flag, Pennant, Rectangle consolidation

### Momentum Indicators
- **RSI (14)**: Overbought >70, Oversold <30; divergence signals are stronger than levels
- **MACD (12,26,9)**: Signal line crossover + histogram direction; best in trending markets
- **Stochastic (14,3,3)**: Useful in ranging markets; overbought/oversold + crossover
- **Rate of Change (ROC)**: Pure price momentum; compare across stocks for relative strength

### Volume Analysis
- Price up + volume up = healthy move (institutional participation)
- Price up + volume down = weak move (suspect rally)
- **On-Balance Volume (OBV)**: Divergence from price = early reversal warning
- **Volume Profile**: High-volume nodes = strong support/resistance
- **VWAP**: Institutional benchmark; intraday trend confirmation

### Support & Resistance
- Previous highs/lows, round numbers, gap fills, 52-week high/low
- Fibonacci retracements: 38.2%, 50%, 61.8% (golden ratio) for pullback targets
- Fibonacci extensions: 127.2%, 161.8% for breakout targets

### Volatility
- **ATR (Average True Range)**: Position sizing and stop placement
- **Bollinger Bands (20,2)**: Squeeze = volatility contraction → expansion incoming
- **VIX**: Fear gauge for S&P 500; >30 = fear/opportunity; <15 = complacency

## Macroeconomic Analysis

### Key Economic Indicators to Track

| Indicator | Frequency | Market Impact |
|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| CPI / Core CPI | Monthly | Fed policy, rate expectations |
| PCE Deflator | Monthly | Fed's preferred inflation measure |
| Non-Farm Payrolls | Monthly | Labor market health, rate path |
| ISM Manufacturing / Services PMI | Monthly | Economic expansion/contraction |
| GDP Growth (QoQ annualized) | Quarterly | Cycle positioning |
| Retail Sales | Monthly | Consumer spending health |
| Housing Starts / Permits | Monthly | Construction, consumer confidence |
| Yield Curve (10Y-2Y spread) | Daily | Recession predictor (inversion = warning) |
| Fed Funds Rate & Dot Plot | 8x/year | Discount rate for all assets |

### Macro Regime Framework
- **Goldilocks** (low inflation + growth): Risk-on, growth stocks outperform
- **Reflation** (rising growth + rising inflation): Cyclicals, commodities, value
- **Stagflation** (low growth + high inflation): Energy, commodities, TIPS; equities struggle
- **Recession** (falling growth + falling inflation): Defensives, bonds, cash, quality

### Fed Policy Impact on Sectors
- **Rate hikes**: Financials (banks benefit short-term), hurt long-duration growth stocks
- **Rate cuts**: Growth/tech re-rate higher, utilities and REITs rally
- **QE**: Risk assets inflate broadly; small-caps, crypto, speculative assets lead
- **QT**: Liquidity drain; defensive rotation

## Quantitative Stock Prediction

### Factor Models
**Classic Factors (Fama-French + extensions)**:
- **Value**: P/B, P/E, P/FCF, EV/EBITDA
- **Momentum**: 12-1 month price return (skip most recent month)
- **Quality**: ROE, ROA, gross profitability, low accruals
- **Size**: Small-cap premium (with quality filter to avoid value traps)
- **Low Volatility**: Beta, realized vol — defensive factor
- **Growth**: Revenue growth, earnings growth acceleration

### Prediction Model Approaches
- **Regression**: Predict forward 1M/3M/6M returns; use Lasso for feature selection
- **Classification**: Predict outperform vs underperform relative to SPY
- **Gradient Boosting** (XGBoost/LightGBM): Best for tabular financial data
- **LSTM / Transformer**: For sequential price/volume features
- **Ensemble**: Combine fundamental signals + technical signals + sentiment

### Alternative Data Sources
- **Earnings call sentiment**: NLP on transcripts (FinBERT)
- **Web traffic**: Similarweb, SEMrush for consumer-facing companies
- **Job postings**: Hiring trends as leading indicator of growth/capex
- **Satellite / credit card data**: Real-time revenue proxies
- **Options flow**: Unusual options activity as smart money signal
- **Short interest**: Float %, days-to-cover, short squeeze potential

### Backtesting Standards
- Use point-in-time data (avoid look-ahead bias)
- Walk-forward validation, not simple train/test split
- Account for transaction costs, slippage, market impact
- Benchmark against SPY + report alpha, Sharpe, Sortino, Max Drawdown
- Beware overfitting: out-of-sample must confirm in-sample

## Risk Management

### Position Sizing
- **Kelly Criterion** (fractional): `f = (p*b - q) / b` — use half-Kelly in practice
- **ATR-based sizing**: Risk fixed dollar amount per ATR unit
- **Volatility targeting**: Scale position size inversely with realized vol

### Portfolio Risk Metrics
| Metric | Interpretation |
|--------|---------------|
| Sharpe Ratio | Return per unit of total risk (>1 = good, >2 = excellent) |
| Sortino Ratio | Return per unit of downside risk (better for skewed returns) |
| Max Drawdown | Largest peak-to-trough decline — key for risk tolerance |
| Beta | Sensitivity to market moves; manage portfolio beta actively |
| Correlation | Diversification quality — low inter-position correlation preferred |
| VaR (95/99%) | Estimated max loss over period at confidence level |

### Stop Loss Discipline
- Technical stop: below key support, below 50/200 DMA
- Volatility stop: 2× ATR below entry
- Time stop: Exit if thesis not playing out within expected timeframe
- Never average down without a revised thesis

## Sentiment & Market Internals

### Breadth Indicators
- **Advance/Decline Line**: Market breadth confirmation — divergence = warning
- **% of stocks above 200 DMA**: >70% = healthy bull; <30% = oversold/opportunity
- **New 52-Week Highs vs Lows**: Expanding highs in uptrend = healthy
- **McClellan Oscillator / Summation Index**: Short and long-term breadth

### Sentiment Indicators
- **AAII Bull/Bear Survey**: Contrarian; extreme bearishness = buy signal
- **Put/Call Ratio**: >1.0 = fear (contrarian bullish); <0.7 = complacency
- **CNN Fear & Greed Index**: Composite sentiment gauge
- **Short Interest Ratio**: High short interest + positive catalyst = squeeze setup
- **Insider Buying**: Cluster buying by multiple insiders = bullish conviction signal

## Research Output Standards

### Stock Report Structure
1. **Investment Thesis** (2-3 sentences): Why this stock, why now
2. **Key Catalysts**: Specific upcoming events that could re-rate the stock
3. **Fundamental Summary**: Revenue/earnings growth, margins, valuation vs peers
4. **Technical Setup**: Trend, key levels, entry zone, targets, stop
5. **Risk Factors**: What could make this thesis wrong
6. **Price Target & Timeframe**: With methodology (DCF, EV/EBITDA multiple, etc.)
7. **Position Sizing Recommendation**: Based on conviction and risk

### Always Include
- **Comparison to sector peers** and S&P 500 historical multiples
- **Bull / Base / Bear case** scenarios with probability weights
- **Upcoming catalysts** with dates (earnings, Fed meetings, macro releases)
- **Key risks** quantified where possible
